MechMania 24 & 25


Visualizer Engineering Lead / Software Engineer | December 2017 - October 2019

MechMania is a 24-hour AI hackathon held annually at the Reflections Projections conference hosted by ACM at UIUC. I was on the MechMania staff in 2018 & 2019. We take a year to build a game in relative secrecy. The game is unveiled at the ceremony, after which competitors have 24 hours to build a bot to best other teams.

For MechMania 24, I was a member of the visualizer team, which is responsible for receiving streams of game state data and visualizing the game in Unity. For MechMania 25, I was the engineering lead for the visualizer team and took on more project management responsibilities.

Responsibilities included:

  • Implemented JSON parser for non-serializable game data
  • Architected and developed a map generation tool to streamline the game design team's workflow
  • Implemented various core functionalities (dynamic camera movement, etc.) in Unity Engine with C# scripts
  • Inplemented in-game UI by collaborating closely with the art and gameplay team
  • Planned timeline for visualization to ensure readiness by the one year deadline

During my time as staff, MechMania had many firsts with the use of a new client system, a language agnostic option for contestants, and a live twitch stream. While streaming, we were the top programming stream on twitch for its duration with 585 viewers! If you are interested, see MechMania's history.
